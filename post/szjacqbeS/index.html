    autonomous
    (of a person 人) able to do things and make decisions without help from anyone else 自主的；有自主权的

    稳定性修饰
    modification for enhanced stability

    state-of-the-art
    using the most modern or advanced techniques or methods; as good as it can be at the present time 应用最先进技术（或方法）的；最先进的
    The system was state of the art. 这一系统是当时最先进的。
    a state-of-the-art system 目前最先进的系统

    State-of-the-art is used with these nouns: computer, electronics, equipment, facility, graphics, machinery, shopping centre, technology

    challenging 替换词 holding back
    2.PHRASAL VERB 短语动词阻挡;阻止 To hold someone or something back means to prevent someone from doing something, or to prevent something from happening.

    【语法信息】：V P n (not pron)

    【语法信息】：V n P
    Stagnation in home sales is holding back economic recovery...
    国内销售的停滞不前正阻碍着经济复苏。
    Jake wanted to wake up, but sleep held him back.
    杰克想要醒来，但睡意使他又沉入梦乡。

    results in 替换词 sparking
    verb

    1. [T] to cause sth to start or develop, especially suddenly 引发；触发 ~ sth
      The proposal would spark a storm of protest around the country. 这一提案将引发全国性的抗议浪潮。
      Winds brought down power lines, sparking a fire. 大风刮断电线，引起了火灾。
      ~ sth off
      The riots were sparked off by the arrest of a local leader. 骚乱是因逮捕一名当地领导人而触发的。

    forefront
    1.N-SING 单数名词显著地位;重要位置;最前沿 If you are at the forefront of a campaign or other activity, you have a leading and influential position in it.

    【搭配模式】：the N
    They have been at the forefront of the campaign for political change.
    他们一直处于政治变革运动的风口浪尖上。
    2.N-SING 单数名词(思考或关注的)重心,焦点 If something is at the forefront of people's minds or attention, they think about it a lot because it is particularly important to them.

    【搭配模式】：the N
    The pension issue was not at the forefront of his mind in the spring of 1985.
    在 1985 年春，养老金问题并非他关注的重点。

    NOTE: 请勿用于商业用途。
